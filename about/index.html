About me

My name is Hayley Zorkic. I am a Senior Computational Biology and Biochemistry student at the University of Texas at Austin with with a Computer Science and Biotechnology minor. Always behind a lab bench, 3D printer, or computer I’m known as the maker, doer, and thinker of my social circles.

My fascination with biotechnology, medicine, and sustainability compel me to make a positive impact on my community and the world. Everyday my knowledge of science and engineering mature, enabling me to work on fun projects with real world applications.

Currently, I work in the lab of Dr. Edward Marcotte investigating uncharacteristized proteins found in cryo-EM images using AlphaFold2, high performance computing environments, and a protein comparision tool called DALI. I am also the Lead Design and Fabrication Engineer at the Cockrell School of Engineerings Texas Inventionworks Makerspcae were I train and consult thousands of students on project development and oversee the digital transformation of the space.

In my free time I enjoy cycling, meditating, cooling, and rollerskating. I am also really enjoy a good podcast.

This website is a slowly updating chronicle of my academic career where I will post my projects, notes, and interesting thoughts. Look around- I hope you find something interesting!
