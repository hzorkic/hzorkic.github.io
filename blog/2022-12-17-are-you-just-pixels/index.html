Are you just pixels?

A concept that’s over-extended it’s stay in my mind for the past year is whether our digital identities are becoming a more valuable than our physcial identities. My personal research on the subject has manifested as many hours of reading articles, writing some poetry, and in the creation of my very first Instagram filter! I coded the filter in javascript, implemented in Spark AR, then finally published to the Instagram filter store.

Intentionally pixelating the user, ‘are you just pixels?’ prompts reflection on the essence of our online interactions with each other and forces the user to interact with a version of themselves and their peers that is less identical to their physical identities- creating a mindful moment of separation between the digital and physical world. Inspired by the blurred boundaries between our real-world selves and digital representations of us, the filter compels users to confront the notion that, while we maintain emotional and physical connections to each other, to the computer, we are ultimately reduced to pixels.

By inviting friends to contribute their photos utilizing the filter, the project became a collaborative exploration of what a network of social connections might look like from a more… computational perspective. Obviously these images do not even begin to capture the essence of these people I love as they are much more than just pixels; furthermore, it’s important to remain cognizant of how much value we give to our digital identities and physical identities relatively.
