üß† Background

During the summer of 2020, I co-led the development of a website to automate and digitize the operational workflow of Texas Inventionworks (TIW) - The University of Texas at Austin’s Makerspace. If you want to learn more about TIW, check out their website here !

When UT Austin shut down all non-essential campus operations during the the initial covid lockdown, my team and I were tasked with creating an application that would enable students to get back into our space, but also serve as a long-standing platform for collection of space utilization data.

Our project goals, given to us by our boss, were to…

  1. Track who is coming into the space, what tools they were using, and for how long for contract tracing.
  2. Create a way to minimize staff-student interaction to avoid the spread of COVID-19.
  3. Build an app students and student staff could continue to use after the pandemic safety measures were lifted in order to gain space usage statistics.

‚ùìProblem Statement

Create an application that enables students to get back on campus to build things while respecting COVID-19 guidelines while serving as a long term space usage data collection tool..

üöß Major Challenges

  1. None of the team members knew how to code.
  2. We needed to design, develop, and deploy the application in less than 3 months before the start of the semester.
  3. We had limited access to student end users.

üß™ Methods

Persona Development

Persona 1, Student: This is a normal university student who uses TIW for classes, research, personal projects, or organizations. Most critically, the student needs to check in and out of TIW when they come as well as check in and out tools as they use them.

Persona 2, Student Staff Member: This is a university student who is formally employed at TIW. Most critically, the student staff member needs to be able to manually check students in and out of TIW when they come as well as check in and out tools as they use to students. The student staff members also need to be able to verify students are trained and able to use special equipment. Student staff members ALSO use the space like normal students do when they are not on shift, so they should have the same privileges.

User Flow

With our user persons developed, we then developed our user flow diagrams which allowed us to align on what experience we needed and wanted the users to have.

Wireframing and Rapid Prototyping

With our user flow complete, we got to brainstorming and figured our end product needed to contain a staff facing application and a student facing application- kind of like a portal connected by backend comprimised by a database and server that let each application interact with one another.

First we developed a wire frame for our application pages to gain a rough idea of functions we wanted as well as where we wanted different components to go on the page (images not inlcuded).

Using the prototyped user flow diagrams and wireframes, we got to building our first protoype, then the next, and a few after that. After many failures, consulting with friends, and self research, we had a MVP deployed and live for end users to test! The tech stack we landed on was the following:

  1. AWS AppSync for the server (-less) server
  2. GraphQL for the API
  3. DynamoDB - the NoSQL, non-relational database
  4. AWS Amplify go host our site
  5. React for the frontend design

Bonus:

  1. Figma for multi-purpose design planning
  2. Agile Scrum group software development

üìà Results

After a few MORE re-builds, we were able to deploy our website to the public right as classes began for the new semester! Our organization was able to open up to the student body and served as the first facility available for undergraduate students to freely do research and projects in during the pandemic. Additionally, we developed a databse that we still use to track student usage behaviors!

Though the software development aspect of this project was technically out of my scope as a Data Scientist and UX Researcher, immersing myself in such a demanding, fast paced project really gave me invaluable insight in the software development life-cycle and allowed me to become intimately familiar with UX methods.
